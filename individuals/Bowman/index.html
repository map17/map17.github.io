Chloe's work examines the duality of our relationship with nature, in particular birds. Her research is rooted in the binary between nature and human construction and the animal as a material object. Using elements of both contemporary taxidermy and Ikebana (Japanese floristry), birds are suspended in ropes, with flowers delicately woven through. The aesthetic of the works is designed to lure the viewer into the darker undertones of the relationships we control and exert.
