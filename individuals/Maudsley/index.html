This installation of a reconstructed archive maps the story of a young woman and her family at the end of the nineteenth century when, during Colonialism, working class women were rendered largely invisible.

'As I told her the story of King Theebaw, of his demands for recompense for their cheating his government, of his objections to their clandestine mining of his country's gems and their illegal annexation of the remains of his country, she grimaced in anguish and twisted the ring set with those gems she had thought of only as a precious heirloom.'
